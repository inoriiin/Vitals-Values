Vitals & Values

A Nurse’s Quick Guide — Know Your Normals

This website was created as a nursing reference for normal and average health data for vital signs.

Newborn (0–1 month)

  • Heart Rate: 100–180 bpm
  • Respiratory Rate: 30–60 breaths/min
  • Blood Pressure: 60–90 / 30–60 mmHg
  • Temperature: 36.5–37.5°C

Infant (1–12 months)

  • Heart Rate: 100–160 bpm
  • Respiratory Rate: 30–53 breaths/min
  • Blood Pressure: 80–100 / 50–65 mmHg
  • Temperature: 36.5–37.5°C

Toddler (1–3 years)

  • Heart Rate: 90–140 bpm
  • Respiratory Rate: 22–37 breaths/min
  • Blood Pressure: 86–106 / 42–63 mmHg
  • Temperature: 36.5–37.5°C

Preschool (3–5 years)

  • Heart Rate: 80–110 bpm
  • Respiratory Rate: 20–28 breaths/min
  • Blood Pressure: 89–112 / 46–72 mmHg
  • Temperature: 36.5–37.5°C

School-age (6–12 yrs)

  • Heart Rate: 75–100 bpm
  • Respiratory Rate: 18–25 breaths/min
  • Blood Pressure: 97–115 / 57–76 mmHg
  • Temperature: 36.5–37.5°C

Adolescent (13–18 yrs)

  • Heart Rate: 60–100 bpm
  • Respiratory Rate: 12–20 breaths/min
  • Blood Pressure: 110–131 / 64–83 mmHg
  • Temperature: 36.5–37.5°C

Adult (19–64 yrs)

  • Heart Rate: 60–100 bpm
  • Respiratory Rate: 12–20 breaths/min
  • Blood Pressure: 90–120 / 60–80 mmHg
  • Temperature: 36.5–37.5°C

Older Adult (65+ yrs)

  • Heart Rate: 60–100 bpm
  • Respiratory Rate: 12–20 breaths/min
  • Blood Pressure: 90–140 / 60–90 mmHg
  • Temperature: 36.0–37.2°C
